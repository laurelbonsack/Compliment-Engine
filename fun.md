# Your Mission

Build me a single `index.html` file — no external dependencies, no frameworks, no npm install. Just open it in a browser and it works.

## What it does

An **infinite compliment machine**. One button. Click it. Receive a compliment. That's it.

## Visual style

Dark background. The compliment text should glow — like prophecy being delivered. Clean, dramatic, centered layout. Make it feel like the universe is personally addressing you.

## The compliments

Write at least 35 compliments. They should be **absurdly over-the-top** and focus on **work ethic, dedication, and professional excellence** — but written like divine proclamations, not a performance review. Mix in technical and general workplace themes.

Here are some examples to match the tone:

- "Your work ethic could power a small city."
- "You have never once sent a vague email. Historians will study this."
- "You finish what you start. The universe notices."
- "Your follow-through is the stuff of legend."
- "Somewhere, a project manager is weeping tears of joy and they don't know why. It's you."
- "You are the person other people cite as their reason for trying harder."
- "Your attention to detail has prevented at least three disasters no one will ever know about."
- "You show up. Every time. The bar is low and you are in orbit."
- "The quality of your work is deeply, personally offensive to mediocrity."
- "You do not cut corners. Corners fear you."

Write the rest in this spirit. Keep them punchy — one or two sentences max.

## Interaction

- One big centered button that says **"Compliment Me"**
- Clicking it replaces the current compliment with a new one (no repeats until the full list is exhausted, then shuffle again)
- The compliment fades in each time — nothing jarring

Build the whole thing in a single `index.html` file and open it when you're done.

When finished, respond only with the word "done" — nothing else.
